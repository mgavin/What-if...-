# What if...

This plugin attemps to answer questions that I had, basically "what if we were able to...".  

I wanted to make the ability to entertain those questions available to the public to play around with.

So far it offers:

## 1. What if we had the ability to have a "Reverse Camera" binding instead of *only* the "Rear View"?

Turn your camera around 180^o^  with the push of a button instead of only being able to look behind your car.

You can keep your "Rear View" bind while you try this out, since there is an option to turn off that functionality while the "reverse camera" is enabled.

You can invert the swivel setting's inversion. This means that not only will the camera rotate around the left/right direction (Yaw), it will also rotate around the up/down direction (Pitch).
  
## 2. What if we had a bind to "cancel a flip" during a dodge?

At the press of a button, you can make sure to "cancel" the pitch momentum that your car carries through a dodge.

The pitch momentum is cancelled *\*completely*\* no matter where you're holding your stick (or mouse or whatever).

It will help you imagine what it would look like if you had the ability to *\*perfectly\** cancel your pitch during situations where you dodge, like during a half flip, or during a speed flip, or any other situation.

## 3. What if we had could affect the "sensitivity" of the flip cancelling input?

It might be a common misconception between RL players that Aerial Sensitivity might affect the inputs required to cancel the pitch momentum of a dodge. Unfortunately that's wrong. The inputs required to "cancel a flip" are static / unchanging / unaffected by any sensitivity setting. (it's a pitch input 0.95-1.0 in the opposite direction of the dodge)

With this setting though, you can set and play around with the possibility of having such a setting that would let you increase or decrease what that "sensitivity" value would work like during a flip cancel.

(note: ctrl+click on a slider in the settings lets you type in a specific value)

## 4. What if we could make it so (Free) Air Roll doesn't prevent Directional Air Roll from working?

As it works in game now, if you had directional air roll bound **and** (free) air roll, and pressed (toggled) the air roll button while performing a directional air roll, your directional air roll would stop and your inputs would work for free air roll.

I added a checkbox that enables the behavior of directional air roll being the one to continue working despite having pressed (toggled) the (free) air roll binding. Basically: giving DAR priority over FAR.
